# How We Write a Blog Post in 30 Minutes

Most content teams take 4-6 hours per post. We do it in 30 minutes.

Not because we're faster writers. Because we eliminated everything that doesn't matter.

## The Process

**Minutes 0-5: The idea**
- What problem did we solve this week?
- What did we learn?
- What would have saved us time?

Don't brainstorm. Don't mind-map. Pick one thing you actually know.

**Minutes 5-10: The outline**
- Hook (one sentence)
- 3-4 main points
- One actionable takeaway
- Done

If you can't explain it simply, you don't know it well enough yet.

**Minutes 10-25: Write**
- Talk like a human
- No jargon
- Short paragraphs
- One idea per paragraph

Don't edit while writing. Just get it down.

**Minutes 25-30: Ship**
- Read it once
- Fix obvious typos
- Hit publish

Perfection is the enemy. Good and published beats perfect and sitting in drafts.

## What We Cut

- ❌ Research (we write what we know)
- ❌ SEO optimization (write for humans, not algorithms)
- ❌ Multiple rounds of editing (one pass, done)
- ❌ Fancy formatting (clean and readable)
- ❌ Editorial calendars (write when you have something to say)

## The Results

We published 12 posts last month. Total time: 6 hours.

Traffic isn't massive, but it's growing. More importantly — we're actually shipping instead of planning to ship.

## For You

Set a timer for 30 minutes. Write about something you learned this week. Don't overthink it. Publish it.

Worst case? You delete it later. Best case? It helps someone.

Either way, you practiced the skill of finishing.
