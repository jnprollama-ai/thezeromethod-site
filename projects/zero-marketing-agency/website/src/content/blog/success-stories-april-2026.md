## Sarah K. — Operations Director, Manufacturing

**The Problem:**
"I was spending 3-4 hours every Monday just catching up on emails from the weekend. By Tuesday, I was already behind."

**The Numbers:**

| Task | Before | After | Time Saved |
|------|--------|-------|------------|
| Executive summaries | 45 min | 8 min | 37 min |
| Weekly reports | 2 hours | 25 min | 1h 35min |
| Email drafting | 90 min/day | 20 min/day | 70 min/day |
| Meeting prep | 1 hour | 15 min | 45 min |

**Weekly savings: 11.5 hours**

**What She Used:**
- The Executive Summary prompt
- Email Framework templates
- Meeting prep automation

**Her Quote:**
> "I used to spend 45 minutes writing executive summaries. Now it takes 8. That's not a feature—it's a refund on my afternoon."

**The Workflow:**
1. Paste meeting notes into AI
2. Use prompt: "Summarize for executives, focus on decisions and action items"
3. Review and send (2-3 min of editing)

## Common Patterns

**What worked across all three:**

1. **They didn't replace thinking—they amplified it**
   - AI handled first drafts, not final decisions
   - Human judgment on strategy, tone, and nuance

2. **They built systems, not one-offs**
   - Saved prompts they reused
   - Created templates for recurring tasks
   - Documented workflows

3. **They started small, then expanded**
   - Sarah started with just email summaries
   - Added one new workflow per week
   - Now runs 15+ AI-assisted workflows

4. **They tracked time religiously**
   - Measured before and after
   - Proved ROI to themselves (and bosses)
   - Identified next opportunities to optimize
