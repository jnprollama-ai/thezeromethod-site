# The 5-Minute Email System That Actually Works

Most people spend 2-3 hours a day on email. That's insane.

Here's the system we use at Zero Method. It works. Not because it's fancy — because it's ruthless.

## The Rule

Every email gets 5 minutes max. If it needs more, it becomes a call or a task. No exceptions.

## The Template

**Minute 1: Scan and delete**
- Delete junk immediately
- Archive anything that doesn't need action
- Only keep what needs a response

**Minutes 2-4: Respond**
- Use the templates below
- Keep it under 3 sentences
- One point per email

**Minute 5: File or schedule**
- Archive it
- Or convert to a task in your system
- Done

## The Templates

**Saying no:**
> "Thanks for thinking of me. I'm not taking on new projects right now. Best of luck."

**Asking for something:**
> "I need [specific thing] by [specific date]. Can you do that?"

**Following up:**
> "Checking in on [thing]. Need an update by [date]."

**Saying yes:**
> "Works for me. [Confirm details]."

## What We Tested

We ran this for 30 days with 12 people. Average email time dropped from 2.5 hours to 45 minutes. Quality didn't suffer. If anything, responses got clearer.

## For You

Try it for one week. Set a timer. Be ruthless about the 5-minute limit. You'll hate it at first. Then you'll love it.
