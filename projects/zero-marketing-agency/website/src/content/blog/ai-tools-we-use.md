# AI Tools We Actually Use (And the Ones We Dropped)

Everyone's got an AI tool list. Most are affiliate links masquerading as recommendations.

Here's what we actually use at Zero Method — and why we dropped the rest.

## Still Using

**Claude (Anthropic)**
- **What we use it for:** Long-form writing, analysis, research
- **Why it stuck:** Actually understands context. Doesn't hallucinate as much.
- **Cost:** $20/month (worth it)

**ChatGPT (OpenAI)**
- **What we use it for:** Quick answers, coding help, brainstorming
- **Why it stuck:** Fast. Good enough for most tasks.
- **Cost:** Free tier works; Pro for heavy use

**Perplexity**
- **What we use it for:** Research, fact-checking
- **Why it stuck:** Cites sources. Saves hours of Googling.
- **Cost:** Free tier is fine

**Notion AI**
- **What we use it for:** Summarizing notes, generating outlines
- **Why it stuck:** Integrated where we already work
- **Cost:** Included with Notion

## We Dropped These

**Jasper**
- **Why:** Expensive. Output was generic. Couldn't tell it was "trained on your brand voice."
- **Verdict:** Overpriced for what it does

**Copy.ai**
- **Why:** Fine for short copy. Falls apart on anything longer than a paragraph.
- **Verdict:** Good for headlines, not content

**Any "AI writer" that promises SEO-optimized posts**
- **Why:** They all sound the same. Google's getting better at detecting it.
- **Verdict:** Waste of money

## The Real Numbers

We tested 14 tools over 90 days. Only 4 made the cut. That's a 71% failure rate — and we're actively looking for good tools.

Most "AI tool roundups" online are just people who signed up for affiliate programs. They haven't actually used them daily.

## For You

Don't chase new tools. Pick 2-3 that work and master them. The tool matters less than the workflow.

**Our stack:**
- Claude for writing
- Perplexity for research  
- Notion for organizing
- ChatGPT for everything else

Total cost: ~$40/month. Replaces what would have been $500+ in contractor time.
